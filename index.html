Instructions

  • Dara is a two-player strategy game played on a 6x5 board.
  • Each player chooses a type of piece (coin, stone, or leaf) and receives 12 pieces to place on the board.
  • The game consists of two phases:
    • Placement Phase: Players take turns placing their pieces, one at a time, in empty spaces on the board. It is not allowed to place more than three pieces of the same type in a row.
    • Movement Phase: Players take turns moving their pieces, one at a time. Pieces can be moved to adjacent spaces, horizontally or vertically, but not diagonally.
  • The objective of the game is to form lines of three or more pieces of the same type. When this happens, the player can capture an opponent's piece.
  • The game ends when a player can no longer make a valid move. The player who can no longer play loses the game.

About Dara

"Dara" is an ancient strategy game that originated in West Africa long before the proliferation of modern board games. This game, often referred to as "Dara" or "Draughts," stands as one of the most notable examples of how games can transcend borders and cultures, carrying with them a rich historical heritage and intellectual challenges. The history of "Dara" dates back centuries, and its strategic complexity continues to captivate players of all ages.
